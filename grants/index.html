Change by Us Philly Civic Innovation Grants

Do you have a great idea for a project that will make Philadelphia smarter, safer or greener? Then apply for a Change by Us Philly Civic Innovation Grant by Month XX, 2012!

Grand awardees will receive
  • Grants from $500 to $3,000
  • Support and technical assistance
  • Public Service Announcement and video content creation support
Eligibility
  • Volunteer-led groups based in neighborhoods throughout Philadelphia. Groups are not required to have 501(c)(3) status. Groups without 501(c)(3) status will need a fiscal sponsor or a bank account if a grant is awarded.
  • Groups must have (or create) a project on the Change by Us Philly website.
  • Groups must have a minimum of 25 members on their Change by Us project page.
  • Individuals, for-profit projects, businesses, and religious, fraternal, and partisan/political organizations are not eligible for Civic Innovation Grants.

How to Apply

Your group already has a Change by Us project page with 25 members:
  • Complete the Civic Innovation Grant application online by April 30, 2012.
Your group does not yet have a project page on Change by Us:
  • Create a project
  • Gather 25 members
  • Complete the Civic Innovation Grant application online by April 30, 2012
All applicants:

You will need the following information to complete the online Civic Innovation Grant application:

Workplan/Timeline: Explain the actions your project will undertake, the people involved, the dates, and intended outcomes. For example, "Paint a wall around a community garden, Sally and Bob Smith, June 30, 2012, neighborhood beautification."

Project Budget: Budget details totaling up to $3,000 that include all planned expenses related to the project. For example, “Paint, 5 gallons, $50.”

Note: Civic Innovation Grants do not cover personnel or ongoing/operating costs. If applicants wish to include a stipend as part of their project budget, the maximum allowance is 25% of their total budget (e.g. for a grant totaling $800, applicants may request up to $200 for a stipend). Budget costs should consist only of one-time expenses related to the project and/or items such as equipment or supplies.

If you have any questions, please contact jeff.friedman@phila.gov for assistance.

Selection Criteria

All project proposals should:
  • Be clear and thoughtful and respond to all questions in the application
  • Result in concrete, measurable, and sustainable projects/initiatives
  • Contribute to building stronger neighborhoods through collaborative effort
  • Track and report key data (e.g. number of volunteers, total volunteer hours)
  • Be innovative and creative

Project Categories:

Proposals should address one (or all) of the following categories. Proposals that fulfill the criteria within a category will be prioritized for an award.

Safer Projects (e.g. public safety)

  • Initiatives that connect neighborhood town watch groups and/or cultivate community action around crime abatement

Smarter Projects (e.g. education)

  • Initiatives should engage and include parents and community members in a meaningful and consequential way.
  • Results of any initiatives should be measurable and quantifiable, specifically as it relates to student achievement and college readiness.
  • Initiatives should be broad-based, inclusive, and equitable.
    • Initiatives should be scalable and replicable, so as to have pervasive, meaningful, and sustainable impact across the city.

Greener Projects (e.g. sustainability or environmentally focused)

  • Tree planting initiatives
  • Creation of neighborhood greenspace, particularly in areas lacking
  • Creation green stormwater infrastructure

Innovation and Creativity

Additional preference will be given to projects that show innovation and creativity in their approach. Examples include projects that cut across several project categories (e.g. using compost or rain water collected within community gardens for tree stewardship) and projects that create unique collaborations (e.g. multi-generational volunteer groups, novel public/private partnerships).

If you have any questions, please contact jeff.friedman@phila.gov.

About Civic Innovation Grants

Through Change by Us Philly, Philadelphians can turn ideas for improving their neighborhoods into action. This grant program, supported by the City of Philadelphia, the Knight Foundation, and CEOs for Cities, intends to provide support for volunteer-led, Change by Us Philly projects that focus on projects that make our city smarter, safer or greener. During this cycle, there will be a total of 20 grants, ranging from $500 to $3,000 each. Funding for grants is made possible by the Knight Foundation and facilitated by CEOs for Cities.
